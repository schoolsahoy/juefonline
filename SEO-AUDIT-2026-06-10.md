# SEO Audit — juefonline.org
**Date:** 2026-06-10 · **Type:** Full site audit (baseline) · **Auditor:** Claude (Fable 5)
**Method:** Scripted on-page/technical analysis of all 15 pages in this repo + live-domain checks + web research on the keyword/competitor landscape. No SEO tool (Ahrefs/Semrush) was connected — volume/difficulty figures are research-informed estimates, not tool data. This report seeds the recurring weekly SEO routine.

---

## Executive Summary

juefonline.org launches from a **clean on-page foundation** — every page has a unique title, meta description, OG tags, exactly one H1, and 100% image alt-text coverage, which is rare for a new site. But it has **one critical performance problem and one critical visibility problem**: the homepage ships **38.9 MB of images** (individual heroes up to 8.3 MB; 229 MB sitewide), which on Indian mobile networks means 30–60-second loads and a failing Largest Contentful Paint — Google demotes slow mobile sites heavily; and the site has **no sitemap, no robots.txt, no canonical tags, and no structured data**, so Google's understanding of the site is left to chance. The blog — the engine that will actually earn rankings — has zero articles.

**Top 3 priorities:** (1) compress/convert all images to WebP at sensible dimensions, (2) ship the indexation pack (sitemap + robots + canonicals + schema + Search Console), (3) start the content engine against the keyword map below.
**Overall assessment: strong foundation, needs work** — the gaps are mechanical and fixable this week; the growth lever is content.

**Strategic insight from the landscape research:** "Shraman education" barely exists as a search category — current SERPs are UPSC exam-prep and Jain philosophy pages, not schools. JUEF is creating the category, which means the brand term must be *defined* by JUEF (a definitional pillar page can own it quickly), while actual traffic must be intercepted from four adjacent demand pools that already exist: **start-a-school intent** (dominated by franchise brands — JEEP's anti-franchise story is a genuine wedge), **gurukul/value-based education intent** (won today by small school blogs — beatable), **AI-age parenting anxiety** (newly hot: CBSE adds AI as a subject from Class 3 in 2026-27), and **Jain community intent** (thin, fragmented SERPs — most ownable). Note: do NOT chase "Jain international school" — those SERPs belong to the unrelated JAIN Group (JIRS Bangalore) and admission directories; wrong brand, wrong intent.

## On-Page Issues
